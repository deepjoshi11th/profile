⚡ JavaScript is BORN! ⚡

Brendan Eich creates LiveScript at Netscape in just 10 days.
It is renamed JavaScript and ships with Netscape Navigator 2.0.

"It's just a little scripting language for the browser."
— Everyone who underestimated it, 1995

✎ Proof That JavaScript Works RIGHT NOW:

What can JavaScript do in 1995?

  • Show alert() boxes (revolutionary!)
  • Validate forms (no more server roundtrips!)
  • Change text on the page (magic!)
  • Annoy people with confirm() dialogs
  • But NOT: fetch data, manipulate DOM trees, or run on servers... yet.
<SCRIPT LANGUAGE="JavaScript">
    // The most revolutionary 3 lines of 1995:
    var name = "World";
    document.write("Hello, " + name + "!");
    alert("Welcome to the programmable web!");
</SCRIPT>

💡 The Binary Birthday Paradox

Deep was supposed to arrive on April 2nd.
But Deep arrived on April 10th.

Why? Because:

(10)2 = (2)10

10 in binary IS 2 in decimal!

Even as a baby, Deep was already thinking in binary.
He arrived on the "right" date —
just in the wrong base.


By 1995, Deep is already 1 year old and probably breaking things.
JavaScript is 0 years old and DEFINITELY breaking things.
The two have a lot in common. Stay tuned.
